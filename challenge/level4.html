🔒 Why Manual Reset?

After any safety event (gate open, E-Stop press), the machine must not auto-restart when the condition clears. IEC 62061 and ISO 13849 require a deliberate operator action — the manual reset — to re-arm the safety circuit. This prevents injuries from unexpected restart after maintenance.

🔴 Fail-Safe NC Wiring

  • E-Stop and Gate sensors are wired NC
  • Normal state: contact closed → PLC reads 1 → safe
  • Wire cut: PLC reads 0 → same as if pressed → machine stops
  • This is "de-energise to trip" — the safe state requires power, not the absence of it

⚡ Safety vs. Operational Channels

  • Safety rungs (1&2): monitor NC sensors only
  • Reset rung (3): gates entry to operational logic
  • Operational rung (4): normal start/stop — cannot override safety
  • Real safety PLCs (Siemens S7-F, Allen-Bradley GuardLogix) enforce this separation in hardware
